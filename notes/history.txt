The Roman Empire was one of the most powerful civilizations in human history, dominating the Mediterranean region for centuries. The Western Roman Empire officially fell in 476 AD when the Germanic chieftain Odoacer deposed the last emperor, Romulus Augustulus.

The Industrial Revolution began in Great Britain during the late 18th century and fundamentally altered global economies. It marked a massive transition from manual labor and agricultural production to mechanized manufacturing, steam power, and urban factories.

The first successful powered airplane flight was achieved by the Wright brothers, Orville and Wilbur, in Kitty Hawk, North Carolina, on December 17, 1903. Their aircraft, the Wright Flyer, stayed airborne for 12 seconds and covered a distance of 120 feet.
